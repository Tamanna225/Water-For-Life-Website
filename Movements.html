Movements related to water

Greywater Recycling

Greywater is wastewater that does not contain sewage, such as water from sinks, showers, and washing machines. By recycling this water, you can use it for irrigation and toilet flushing, which reduces your dependence on freshwater sources.


World Water Day

World Water Day is celebrated annually on March 22nd and is aimed at raising awareness about the importance of water conservation and the need to address water-related challenges around the world.


Save Our Water

Save Our Water is a campaign launched by the California Department of Water Resources to encourage water conservation and provide tips and resources to help Californians reduce their water usage.


40 Gallon Challenge

The 40 Gallon Challenge is a national campaign that challenges individuals and families to reduce their water usage by 40 gallons per day.


WaterSense

WaterSense is a program sponsored by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) that promotes water efficiency and provides information and resources to help consumers make smart water choices.


Tap Project

The Tap Project is a campaign launched by UNICEF to provide access to safe drinking water to children around the world. During World Water Week, participating restaurants ask their customers to donate $1 for the tap water they would normally receive for free, with the proceeds going to support UNICEF's water programs


Blue Movement

The Blue Movement is a global initiative launched by the World Wildlife Fund to protect freshwater resources and promote sustainable water management practices. The initiative works to conserve freshwater ecosystems, reduce water pollution, and promote sustainable water use by businesses and individuals.
